# Patient Personas — Huntington Beach Prosthodontics

These personas drive all ad copy, landing page messaging, keyword targeting, and content decisions.
Each persona reflects a real segment of patients who book implant consultations.

---

## Persona 1: The Quality Seeker

**Name archetype:** Karen or David, 45–65  
**Occupation:** Professional, retired executive, business owner  
**Income:** $120k+  
**Tech comfort:** Moderate — researches on desktop, reads reviews carefully

### What They Want
They've already decided they want implants. Their primary concern is getting it done *right* — by someone qualified, not just the cheapest provider they can find. They've likely been burned by over-promised dental work before, or they know someone who has. They are willing to pay more for a specialist.

### Core Pain Points
- Fear of getting an implant that looks fake or fails prematurely
- Distrust of general dentists doing specialty work "on the side"
- Want one provider who owns the whole process, not a chain of referrals
- Frustrated that it's hard to tell who is actually qualified

### How They Search
- `"prosthodontist dental implants"`
- `"board certified implant dentist huntington beach"`
- `"implant specialist near me"`
- `"prosthodontist vs general dentist implants"`

### What Converts Them
- Credentials front and center (board-certified, DDS, MS, specialty training)
- Explanation of *why* a prosthodontist is different (not just "we're the best")
- Dr. Cheong's photo and personal bio
- Patient reviews that mention expertise and outcomes

`/lp/dental-implants`

### Ad Hook
> "Board-Certified Implant Specialist in HB | Prosthodontist-Designed | Free Consultation"

## Persona 2: The Gap Patient

**Name archetype:** Mike or Sarah, 35–55  
**Occupation:** Working professional, parent  
**Income:** $70k–$120k  
**Tech comfort:** High — compares options across multiple sites, reads Reddit/forums

### What They Want
They had a tooth extracted (often months or years ago) and have been living with the gap. It bothers them aesthetically and they've started reading about bone loss. They're comparing implants vs. bridges vs. doing nothing. They're not in acute pain — it's more of a simmering concern that finally pushed them to research.

### Core Pain Points
- Embarrassed by the gap, especially in social situations
- Worried about long-term jawbone deterioration they've read about
- Confused about their options (implant vs. bridge vs. flipper)
- Nervous about the surgery and recovery

### How They Search
- `"dental implant for one missing tooth"`
- `"replace missing tooth options"`
- `"tooth implant vs bridge"`
- `"single tooth implant cost"`
- `"how long does a dental implant take"`

### What Converts Them
- Clear comparison of options (implant vs. bridge) that positions implant as the long-term winner
- Before/after photos of single tooth cases
- Step-by-step process explanation that demystifies the procedure
- Timeline and recovery expectations

### Landing Page
`/lp/dental-implants`

### Ad Hook
> "Replace That Missing Tooth Permanently | Natural-Looking Implant | Free Consult HB"

## Persona 3: The Denture Refugee

**Name archetype:** Linda or Bob, 60–78  
**Occupation:** Retired or semi-retired  
**Income:** $50k–$100k (often fixed income but willing to spend for quality of life)  
**Tech comfort:** Low to moderate — likely on mobile, may need family help researching

### What They Want
They've worn dentures for years and hate them. They've adapted their entire life around the limitations — what they eat, when they laugh, how they interact socially. They've heard implants exist but assumed they couldn't afford them or weren't a candidate. Something recently tipped them over the edge (a denture broke, a grandchild's wedding is coming up, a friend got implants).

### Core Pain Points
- Deep embarrassment and loss of dignity around eating in public
- Dentures slip, click, or fall out at the worst moments
- Limited diet — can't eat certain foods they love
- Assume they're too old or bone is too far gone (fear of disqualification)
- Don't understand financing options — assume it's a cash-only luxury

### How They Search
- `"alternatives to dentures"`
- `"permanent dentures implants"`
- `"implants for denture wearers"`
- `"tired of my dentures"`
- `"snap in dentures"`
- `"full arch dental implants"`

### What Converts Them
- Direct acknowledgment of denture frustrations ("no more slipping")
- Clear before/after or lifestyle imagery
- Explicit reassurance that bone loss doesn't automatically disqualify them
- Financing options visible early (not buried)
- Simple, warm language — not clinical jargon

### Landing Page
`/lp/full-arch-restoration`

### Ad Hook
> "Done With Dentures? Permanent Fixed Implants in HB | Free Evaluation"

## Persona 4: The All-on-X Candidate

**Name archetype:** Tom or Patricia, 50–70  
**Occupation:** Trade worker, manager, homemaker  
**Income:** $60k–$130k  
**Tech comfort:** Moderate — has already researched All-on-4 specifically, watched YouTube videos

### What They Want
They have multiple failing or missing teeth — often a combination of decay, extractions, and remaining compromised teeth. They've done enough research to know "All-on-4" is a thing. They may have already gotten a quote somewhere and found it expensive. They want a second opinion, or they want to find the right specialist to do it. They are serious buyers with high intent.

### Core Pain Points
- Multiple failing teeth making eating and smiling painful or embarrassing
- Overwhelmed by conflicting information about All-on-4 vs All-on-6 vs other options
- Worried about cost — the sticker price shocked them
- Not sure if their bone situation makes them eligible
- Want to leave with teeth the same day (scared of a "gap phase")

### How They Search
- `"all on 4 dental implants"`
- `"all on 4 cost huntington beach"`
- `"all on 4 vs all on 6"`
- `"same day teeth implants"`
- `"full mouth dental implants"`
- `"all on x near me"`

### What Converts Them
- Same-day teeth explicitly mentioned
- Prosthodontist-designed (vs. general dentist) as a differentiator
- Clear process walkthrough (consult → surgery → final arch)
- Financing options with monthly payment framing
- Social proof from full-arch cases

## Persona 5: The Cost Researcher

**Name archetype:** Alex or Jennifer, 30–55  
**Occupation:** Any — income-sensitive  
**Income:** $50k–$90k  
**Tech comfort:** High — comparison shopping, reading forums, price-anchoring

### What They Want
They want dental implants but the price they've seen quoted online ($3,000–$6,000 per tooth) has triggered sticker shock. Before committing to a consultation, they need to understand what the number actually includes, whether insurance covers any of it, and whether financing is available. They're not necessarily looking for the cheapest option — they're looking for transparency and to feel like they're not being taken advantage of.

### Core Pain Points
- Online prices vary wildly and nobody explains why
- Fear of hidden fees or costs that escalate after they commit
- Don't know if their insurance covers any of it
- Have heard horror stories about cheap implant providers
- Want to feel financially in control before booking

### How They Search
- `"how much do dental implants cost"`
- `"dental implant price huntington beach"`
- `"dental implants with insurance"`
- `"affordable dental implants near me"`
- `"dental implant financing"`
- `"dental implant payment plan"`

### What Converts Them
- Transparent cost breakdown (what's actually included)
- Explicit mention of financing (CareCredit, 0% plans)
- Insurance coverage explanation
- "No hidden fees" or "get a written estimate" messaging
- The specialist framing as a *value* argument, not a luxury upsell

### Landing Page
`/lp/implant-cost`

### Ad Hook
> "Dental Implant Cost HB | Transparent Pricing + Financing | Free Estimate — No Surprises"
