# Photography Shot List — Huntington Beach Prosthodontics

**Prepared:** March 2026
**Purpose:** Comprehensive shot list for professional photographer to capture all images needed for website, Google Business Profile, Facebook, and Instagram.

---

## How to Use This Document

This is organized by **shooting location/setup** so the photographer can work efficiently without constantly moving between setups. Each shot has a unique **ID** (e.g., `FAC-01`) used in the companion [Photo Placement Map](./photography-placement-map.md) to show exactly where each image will be used.

**Priority Key:**
- **P1** = Must-have (critical gaps on website)
- **P2** = High value (significantly improves pages)
- **P3** = Nice-to-have (extra assets for future use / social content)

## Shooting Day Notes for Photographer

### Recommended Schedule (Full Day)
1. **Early morning (golden hour):** Exterior shots (FAC-01 through FAC-07)
2. **Morning:** Empty office interiors — treatment rooms, reception, hallways (REC, TRM series)
3. **Mid-morning:** Doctor portraits and team photos (DOC, TEM series)
4. **Late morning:** Clinical close-ups and product shots (CLN series)
5. **Afternoon:** Doctor-patient interactions and consultation scenes (DPI, CON series)
6. **Afternoon:** Patient smile photos / before-afters (SMI series — schedule consented patients)
7. **Late afternoon / golden hour:** Lifestyle and outdoor shots (LIF series)
8. **Throughout day:** Social media candids (SOC series)

### Technical Notes
- Deliver in both high-res (print/original) and web-optimized (.webp, 2000px wide max)
- Landscape shots: minimum 2400 x 1600px
- Portrait shots: minimum 1200 x 1600px
- Square shots: minimum 1600 x 1600px
- Color profile: sRGB for web
- Style: bright, clean, modern — not overly clinical or cold
- Avoid harsh shadows and fluorescent color casts in clinical areas
- Patient faces require signed photo release forms
